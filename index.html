Air Compressor

Air compressors are used for increasing gas pressure by reducing its volume. They are also used to force atmospheric air under pressure and can store it in a tank to use it later. The pressures starts building up when compressed air is released converting potential energy to kinetic energy.

Absorption Tower

An absorption tower is a large tank that absorbs gas. It allows gaseous phases pass into liquid phases. Additionally, adsorption columns are used. They are where particles stick to a solid's surface without going through it, it stays there with intermolecular forces. Adsorptions are concentrations in fluid phase and solid adsorbent change overtime and their position in the fixed bed, as adsorption proceeds.

Agitated Vessel

Agitated vessels exchange heat and are mostly used in lots of chemical and biochemical process industries. The mass transfer for the most part involves dispersed gases absorbing into and often reacting with an ongoing liquid phase. This giant cylindrical tank is driven by an electric motor that rotates the impellers within the vessel.

Centrifuge

Centrifuges apply a sustained centrifugal force - due to rotation. Centrifuges are used to separate liquids/fluids and gases, depending on their density. They rotate at very high and fast speeds, separating substances using the power of centripetal force.
